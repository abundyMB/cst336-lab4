Welcome to Earth!



Earth, home of the human race, third planet from the sun, is the only planet known to host life. Only a handful of people have ever left its thin atmospheric envelope. Roughly 70% of the Earth's surface is covered in water, and while we are familiar with its surface, 95% of the ocean depths remain unexplored, leaving untold numbers of discoveries and surprises waiting for us in the deep.


Fun facts

Moon Size and Formation:

The moon is one of the strangest in the Solar System. Most planets' moons are only a small fraction their size, and yet the Earch's moon is 25% the size of its host. The only other large body with such an abnormal size ratio is Pluto and its moon Charon. Like Mercury, the Earth likely suffered a giant impact by a Mars-sized protoplanet called Theia, remelting the Earth's surface and sending debris flying into space, which eventually formed our Moon.

Composition:

Earth is the densest planet in the Solar System (perhaps more than just compositionally), just edging out Mercury with 5.51 grams per cubic centimeter. It is also the only planet with tectonic planets, which work to recycle the crust and mantle, refreshing the surface and erasing old impact scars, among other surface features. This tectonic activity is also responsible for a surprising find: that there is as much water in the Earth's mantle as there is in all of the oceans combined.
