Fun facts:

Size:

Most people believe that the Solar System ends at its barrier with interstellar space, roughly 12 billion miles from the sun. Voyager 1 and 2 have both left the heliosphere, where the solar wind butts up against the interstellar medium. However, the sun's influence extends much, much farther. Around the sun is a massive sphere of comets, the Oort Cloud, stretching up to a trillion miles, or 25% of the way to the nearest star.
Oort Cloud: In Depth


The Sun, King of Mystery:

It's understood that the sun is the dominant force in the Solar System, since everything orbits it. The sheer size of the sun can be difficult to grasp. In terms of mass, it makes up 99.85% the mass of the Solar System. A million Earths could fit inside it. And yet, there is so much we don't know about the sun. However, with NASA's Parker Solar Probe and Solar Orbiter getting closer to the sun than any spacecraft before them, scientists hope to shed light on our star's many mysteries.
Solar Orbiter: Closest ever pictures taken of the Sun


Pluto, the Most Surprising Body of All:

In 2015, NASA's New Horizons spacecraft flew 8000 miles above the dwarf planet's surface and stunned the world. Scientists had been expecting a cold, featureless desert, and instead found a world more varied and active than almost any other body in the Solar System. A lava-lamp like heart, haze layers, and an underground ocean are but a few of the wonders of this beloved world.
