Welcome to Venus!



Venus, the second planet from the sun, is one of the most Earth-like planets in the Solar System. However, while Earth and Venus were likely twin planets for most of their lives, Venus's evolution took a radical turn about a billion years ago. Perhaps the result of volcanic eruptions flooding the atmosphere with greenhouse gases, Venus today now hosts the hotest surface temperatures in the Solar System (over 860 F), an atmosphere pressure 90 times that of Earth's, and an atomospheric partly composed of sulfuric acid. It's no wonder then why Venus is considered Earth's evil twin.


Fun facts

Strange Rotation:

Unlike most of the other planets, Venus rotates in an opposite direction from the rest of the planets. Additionaly, a day there is longer than a year (243 Earth-days per Venusian day, 224 Earth-days per Venusian year).

Potential Life:

Temperatures hot enough to melt lead. A crushing, acidic atmosphere. These hellish conditions seem like the last place one would find life. Yet, some scientists suggest that life could survive on Venus. Or rather, in Venus's clouds. The planet's upper atmosphere offers a much more hospitable environment conducive to lifeforms that ride air currents and stay above the deadly surface.
