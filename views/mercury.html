Welcome to Mercury!



Mercury is the planet closest the sun, with a year lasting a little less than 90 days. The innermost planet is known for its scorching heat and desolate appearance, but as this world has been studied further, it has revealed astonishing surprises.


Fun facts

Ice:

Even though the surface temperature can be up to 800 F, water ice has been found in several of its polar craters, places untouched by the sun for billions of years. This is due to the lack of atmosphere, which prevents the scorching heat from reaching these ice pockets, and also results in the night side hosting freezing temperatures of several hundred degrees below zero.
Ice on Mercury


Core:

While Mercury has many strange features, one of its weirdest is its core. Most planets' cores are small in comparison to the rest of its mass, but Mercury's core takes up 55% of its volume. Multiple theories have been put forth, but the most popular is the Giant Impact Hypothesis, which claims an object 1/6 its mass slammed into it and stripped away its outer layers.
